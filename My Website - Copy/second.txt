<h2 style="color: #ff9999">The basic components of cosmetics</h2>
<h3 style="color: #ff9999">The cosmetic formulas differ slightly from each other, and most of them contain a mixture of the following basic ingredients:</h3>
<ul style="list-style-type:square"><img src="water.jpg" alt="" align="right" style="width:200px;height:200px;"><img src="Preservatives.jpg" alt="" align="right" style="width:200px;height:200px;">

<li style="color:white"><b>Water:</b>is the basis for almost all types of cosmetics, such as creams, lotions, cosmetics, deodorants, shampoos, and hair conditioners,They are used as solvents for other materials, to form emulsions for consistency, and the materials used in cosmetics are distilled and purified water, free from germs and contaminants.</li>
<li style="color:white"><b>Emulsifiers:</b>can be defined as the components that help to protect various substances such as oil and water from separation, and many cosmetics depend on them.</li>
<li style="color:white"><b>Preservatives:</b> It is an important component that is added to cosmetics to increase their shelf life, and to prevent the growth of microorganisms such as bacteria and fungi that may cause product spoilage and damage to the user, and most preservatives are soluble in water; because most microbes live in water, and materials can Preservatives used in cosmetics to be natural or synthetic, and preservatives used include paraben, benzyl alcohol, salicylic acid, and formaldehyde.</li>

<li style="color:white"><b>Emollients:</b> soften the skin to prevent water loss, and are used in many cosmetics.</li>
<li style="color:white"><b>Materials:</b> that are used as emollients: beeswax, olive oil, coconut oil, lanolin, petroleum jelly, mineral oil, glycerin, and zinc oxide.
<li style="color:white"><b>Dyes or colourants:</b> colourants can be mineral components such as iron oxide, mica flakes, manganese, chromium oxide, and tar, and some plants, such as beet powder, or animals, such as scarlet insect that are often used in red lipstick, are also sourced.</li>
<li style="color:white"><b>Fresheners:</b> They are chemicals, which may be natural or synthetic, added to cosmetics to give them an attractive scent. Capacitors: They are added to cosmetics to give them the required density, and they come in four chemical families: fatty capacitors, which include: cetyl alcohol, citric acid, carnauba wax, naturally produced capacitors, such as hydroxyethyl cellulose, guar gum, xanthan gum, gelatin, and capacitors Minerals like magnesium and aluminum silicate, silica, bentonite, and synthetic condensate are often used in creams and moisturizers.</li>
